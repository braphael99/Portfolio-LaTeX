\subsection{Problem \ref{BFSDFS}}
\begin{required} \label{BFSDFS}

For the graph drawn below, give an ordering of nodes that will be 
explored by (1) BFS (2 points) and (2) DFS (2 points) when starting from node $A$.

\begin{center}
\tikz{
    \tikzstyle{default}=[circle,top color = white, draw];
    \node (A) at (0,2) [default] {$A$};
    \node (B) at (2,4) [default] {$B$};
    \node (C) at (2,0) [default] {$C$};
    \node (D) at (4,2) [default] {$D$};
    \node (E) at (6,4) [default] {$E$};
    \node (F) at (6,0) [default] {$F$};
    \node (G) at (8,2) [default] {$G$};

    \draw (A) -- (B);
    \draw (A) -- (C);

    \draw (B) -- (C);
    \draw (B) -- (D);
    \draw (B) -- (E);

    \draw (C) -- (D);
    \draw (C) -- (F);

    \draw (D) -- (E);
    \draw (D) -- (F);

    \draw (E) -- (F);
    \draw (E) -- (G);

    \draw (F) -- (G);
}
\end{center}

\begin{proof}[Answer]
BFS will explore the graph in the following order (assuming we are pushing onto queue in alphabetical order):\\
1.) A marked as visited\\
2.) B and C marked as visited\\
3.) D and E marked as visited\\
4.) F marked as visited\\
5.) G marked as visited\\
6.) BFS stops as all nodes visited now\\
DFS will explore the graph in the following order (also assuming alphabetical order to push onto stack):\\
1.) A marked as visited\\
2.) B marked as visited\\
3.) C marked as visited\\
4.) D marked as visited\\
5.) E marked as visited\\
6.) F marked as visited\\
7.) G marked as visited\\
8.) DFS stops as all nodes have been explored.\\
\end{proof}
